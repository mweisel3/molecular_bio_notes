DNA Structure and Topology

▶What is the genetic material?

DNA as the Genetic Material

  • The Hershey-Chase experiment (1952) provided key evidence that DNA, not protein, is the genetic material.
  • They used bacteriophage T2, a virus that infects bacteria.
  • Phages were labeled with radioactive isotopes: ³⁵S for protein and ³²P for DNA.
  • They found that the ³²P (DNA) entered the bacterial cells, while the ³⁵S (protein) remained outside. Conclusion: DNA carries the genetic information necessary for viral replication.
▶What are the building blocks of DNA?

The Structure of DNA

  • DNA is a polymer made of nucleotides.
  • Each nucleotide consists of three components:
  1. A deoxyribose sugar
  2. A phosphate group
  3. A nitrogenous base
  • Bases: - Purines (double ring): Adenine (A) and Guanine (G)
  • Pyrimidines (single ring): Cytosine (C) and Thymine (T)
  • A nucleoside is a sugar and a base, without the phosphate group.

Nucleoside is the sugar and base (without the phosphate group backbone) Nucleotide is sugar, base, and phosphate group backbone

▶How was the structure of DNA discovered?

Discovery of the Double Helix

  • Rosalind Franklin's X-ray diffraction image ("Photo 51") was critical. It suggested a helical structure with a uniform width.
  • Erwin Chargaff's rules: - Amount of A = Amount of T - Amount of G = Amount of C - This implies that A pairs with T, and G pairs with C.
  • Watson and Crick (1953) used this information to build their model of the DNA double helix.
▶What is Chargaff's rule?

The number of A=T and C=G

▶What are the key features of the DNA Double Helix?

Features of the B-DNA Double Helix

  • Two polynucleotide strands coiled around each other to form a right-handed helix.
  • The strands are antiparallel (run in opposite directions, 5' to 3' and 3' to 5').
  • The sugar-phosphate backbones are on the outside, and the bases are on the inside.
  • Complementary base pairing:
    - Adenine (A) pairs with Thymine (T) via 2 hydrogen bonds.
  • Guanine (G) pairs with Cytosine (C) via 3 hydrogen bonds.
  • The helix has a major groove and a minor groove, which are important for protein binding.
  • One complete turn of the helix is 34 Å (3.4 nm) and contains about 10.5 base pairs.
▶How does DNA replicate?

DNA Replication Model

  • There were three proposed models: conservative, dispersive, and semi-conservative.
  • The Meselson-Stahl experiment (1958) proved the semi-conservative model.
  • Experiment:
  1. E. coli were grown in a medium with a heavy isotope of nitrogen (¹⁵N).
  2. They were then transferred to a medium with a lighter isotope (¹⁴N).
  3. DNA was sampled after each generation and separated by density centrifugation.
  • Results:
  • After one generation, all DNA was of intermediate density (a hybrid of ¹⁵N and ¹⁴N). This ruled out the conservative model.
  • After two generations, there were two bands: one intermediate and one light (¹⁴N). This ruled out the dispersive model. Conclusion: Each new DNA molecule consists of one old strand and one newly synthesized strand
▶What is DNA denaturation?

DNA Denaturation and Renaturation

  • Denaturation (Melting): The separation of the two DNA strands, caused by high temperature or extreme pH. The hydrogen bonds break.
  • The melting temperature (Tm) is the temperature at which 50% of the DNA is denatured.
  • Tm is higher for DNA with a higher G-C content because G-C pairs have 3 hydrogen bonds, making them more stable.
  • Renaturation (Annealing): The process of complementary single strands pairing back together upon cooling.

Summary

The Hershey-Chase experiment established that DNA is the genetic material. Watson and Crick, using data from Franklin and Chargaff, determined that DNA is an antiparallel, right-handed double helix with complementary base pairing (A-T and G-C). The structure has major and minor grooves for protein interactions and exists in different forms (A, B, Z). The Meselson-Stahl experiment demonstrated that DNA replicates semi-conservatively, where each new DNA molecule contains one original and one new strand.
